blended|Blended Whisky|Blended whisky is often the style many people in Korea first picture when they think of premium whisky. Master blenders combine spirits from multiple distilleries to create a balanced and reliable profile, making this style approachable for beginners and still satisfying for experienced drinkers.|Chivas Regal, Dewar's, Hibiki
bourbon|Bourbon|Bourbon is built on a mash bill with at least 51 percent corn, giving it a bold character with sweetness and weight. New charred oak barrels add strong vanilla, oak, and impact, which helps bourbon stand apart from lighter fruit- or flower-driven styles.|Wild Turkey, Buffalo Trace, Maker's Mark
peat|Peated Whisky|Peated whisky, especially from Islay and nearby regions of Scotland, is known for smoky intensity shaped by peat fire and coastal aging. Its bold smoke and maritime character can divide opinion, but for people who love it, few other styles can replace it.|Ardbeg, Lagavulin, Laphroaig
sherry_cask|Sherry Cask Whisky|Sherry cask whisky carries the depth of casks that once held sherry wine. It is typically rich and weighty, often showing a deep amber color with notes of dried fruit, dense chocolate, and layered sweetness, which is why many drinkers see it as one of the most luxurious whisky styles.|The Macallan, Glendronach, Tamdhu
bourbon_cask|Bourbon Cask Whisky|Bourbon cask whisky is matured in barrels that previously held bourbon. It keeps the vanilla sweetness associated with bourbon oak while adding brighter and lighter fruit notes, making it a cleaner and more vibrant contrast to the heavier style often associated with sherry casks.|Glenmorangie Original, The Glen Grant Arboralis, Balvenie DoubleWood 12 Years Old
independent_bottling|Independent Bottling|Independent bottlers select casks from different distilleries and release them under their own philosophy, often with distinctive design and strong character. Because quantities are usually small, these bottles feel rare and appeal to drinkers who want something more personal, collectible, and unusual.|Elements of Islay, Signatory Vintage, Gordon & MacPhail
